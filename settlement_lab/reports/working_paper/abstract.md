# Abstract

This paper proposes a measurement framework for the economics of global settlement — how payment systems transform monetary claims into usable economic value, and what is lost when that transformation is delayed, uncertain, or liquidity-intensive.

We introduce five corridor- and rail-level indices: the Settlement Drag Index (SDI), Operational Liquidity Burden (OLB), Finality Quality Index (FQI), Payment Network Fragility Model (PNF), and Payment Friction Incidence Model (PFI). The framework is built from an institutional treasury perspective emphasizing prefunding, settlement finality, FX exposure during settlement windows, and operational liquidity traps.

Under this specification, the lab **measures** settlement economics; it does **not** establish causal welfare effects without further identification. This is research infrastructure — not financial advice, not investment recommendation, and not operational guidance for live payment systems.
