# Abstract

**When Settlement Windows Collapse: Stablecoins, Finality, and the Redistribution of Payment Risk**

This paper proposes a measurement framework for stablecoin settlement economics — how tokenized dollar instruments compress ledger settlement windows while potentially relocating liquidity, compliance, legal, and run-speed risk to issuers, reserve markets, and off-ramp infrastructure.

We introduce eight research constructs: Stablecoin Finality Quality Index (SFQI), Settlement Window Compression (SWC), Stablecoin Liquidity Transformation (SLT), Digital Run Velocity (DRV), Stablecoin Dollarization Index, Tokenized Money Singleness Index, Compliance Settlement Drag, and Stablecoin Value Survival Index (SVSI). The framework is built from an institutional treasury perspective emphasizing the gap between **ledger finality** and **economic finality**.

Under this specification, the lab **measures** settlement-window compression and risk redistribution; it does **not** establish causal welfare effects without further identification. This is research infrastructure — not financial advice, not investment recommendation, and not operational guidance for live payment systems.
