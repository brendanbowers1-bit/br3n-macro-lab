# Introduction (Draft)

Payment systems transform monetary claims into usable economic value. Treasury operations — settlement cutoffs, liquidity buffers, prefunding, FX exposure during settlement windows, and reconciliation — determine whether a transfer is economically final, not merely confirmed on a ledger.

Stablecoins promise near-instant ledger settlement. This lab asks: **when settlement windows collapse toward zero, where does risk go?**

## Core thesis

Stablecoins do not eliminate settlement risk; they change its location, speed, and legal form.

## Research scope

- Ledger vs economic finality
- Settlement window compression and risk redistribution
- Issuer reserve liquidity transformation
- Digital run conditions (not run prediction)
- Retail dollarization outside banking
- Singleness of private tokenized money
- Compliance as the real settlement window
- Value survival on remittance corridors

Built from a Visa Treasury–informed institutional perspective — not trading signals or speculative coin analysis.
