# Project Overview

## Mission

The Metastable Hydride Superconductor Initiative explores whether metastable hydride phases can be identified, modeled, and prioritized as candidate systems for high-temperature or ambient-pressure superconductivity research.

## Scientific Motivation

Hydride superconductors have shown that hydrogen-rich materials can host strong electron-phonon coupling and high superconducting transition temperatures, often under extreme pressure. The challenge is whether hydrogen-rich phases can be stabilized, trapped, quenched, or accessed at lower pressure or ambient conditions.

This initiative focuses on metastable hydride pathways rather than equilibrium-only materials discovery.

## Flagship Candidate Pathway

**Mg₂IrH₅ → Mg₂IrH₆**

The project treats Mg₂IrH₅ as a possible parent hydride system and Mg₂IrH₆ as a hypothetical hydrogen-inserted derivative to evaluate computationally.

The pathway is **not assumed to be valid**. It is a test case for a broader platform:

1. identify hydrogen-deficient parent hydride,
2. propose vacancy-filled derivative,
3. compute structural stability,
4. compute hydrogen insertion energetics,
5. evaluate phonons,
6. evaluate electron-phonon coupling if stable,
7. assess synthesis feasibility,
8. require independent validation.

## Platform Concept

The broader platform is not limited to Mg-Ir-H.

The platform can screen candidate hydrides where:

- a known lower-hydrogen phase exists,
- a plausible higher-hydrogen derivative can be proposed,
- the derivative may be metastable,
- hydrogen insertion barriers may be accessible,
- pressure/quench or chemical routes may stabilize the phase.

## Research Standards

The initiative separates:

- hypothesis,
- computational candidate,
- dynamically stable candidate,
- electronically interesting candidate,
- experimentally plausible candidate,
- independently replicated result.

No candidate should be presented as a discovery until it has passed appropriate validation.
