# Technical Brief

## Working Title

Metastable Hydride Superconductivity via Non-Equilibrium Hydrogen Insertion: A Mg₂IrH₅ → Mg₂IrH₆ Proof-of-Platform Study

## Core Hypothesis

Hydrogen-deficient hydrides may contain structural motifs that can be computationally targeted for non-equilibrium hydrogen insertion or vacancy filling. Some hydrogen-enriched derivative phases may be metastable and may possess electronic or phonon properties relevant to superconductivity.

## Initial Target System

**Parent:** Mg₂IrH₅

**Hypothetical derivative:** Mg₂IrH₆

**Research question:** Can the hypothetical Mg₂IrH₆-like derivative be structurally, dynamically, and electronically plausible enough to justify deeper computational and experimental study?

## Required Computational Tests

### 1. Structure preparation

- obtain or construct Mg₂IrH₅ parent structure
- identify hydrogen vacancy or insertion sites
- construct candidate Mg₂IrH₆ derivative structures
- relax structures using DFT

### 2. Thermodynamic screening

- calculate total energies
- compare formation enthalpies
- evaluate decomposition pathways
- compare against competing Mg-Ir-H phases

### 3. Dynamic stability

- calculate phonon dispersion
- check for imaginary phonon modes
- reject dynamically unstable candidates or investigate distortions

### 4. Hydrogen insertion energetics

- compute energy difference between parent and derivative
- evaluate hydrogen chemical potential assumptions
- estimate insertion feasibility

### 5. Kinetic pathway

- use NEB or related methods to estimate hydrogen migration/insertion barriers
- identify whether a metastable pathway is plausible

### 6. Electronic structure

- calculate density of states
- evaluate Fermi-level states
- identify hydrogen/metal contributions near Fermi level

### 7. Superconductivity screening

If and only if the candidate is structurally and dynamically plausible:

- calculate electron-phonon coupling
- estimate Tc using appropriate approximations
- clearly label uncertainty

## Required Experimental Review

Before any experimental outreach:

- validate safety requirements for hydrides and hydrogen handling
- identify synthesis routes
- assess pressure, temperature, and hydrogen source requirements
- evaluate whether target phase could be quenched
- define characterization plan
- require independent replication

## Scientific Caution

This project does **not** claim superconductivity. It proposes a structured pathway for evaluating metastable hydride candidates.
