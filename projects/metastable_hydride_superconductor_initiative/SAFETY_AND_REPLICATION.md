# Safety and Replication Standards

## Safety First

Hydride chemistry, hydrogen handling, high-pressure synthesis, and transition-metal compounds can involve serious hazards.

**This initiative does not provide do-it-yourself synthesis instructions.**

Any experimental work must be performed only by qualified researchers in properly equipped laboratories under institutional safety protocols.

## Required Safety Review

Before experimental work:

- chemical hazard review
- hydrogen handling review
- high-pressure equipment review
- reactive hydride review
- toxic metal compound review
- waste disposal review
- emergency procedure review
- institutional approval

## Replication Standard

A candidate result should move through these levels:

1. computational hypothesis
2. computationally relaxed candidate
3. dynamically stable candidate
4. experimentally synthesized phase
5. structurally characterized phase
6. measured physical property
7. independently replicated result

## Superconductivity Claim Standard

A superconductivity claim should require:

- structural identification of the claimed phase
- zero resistance or strong transport evidence
- magnetic screening / Meissner evidence
- impurity exclusion
- repeatability
- independent replication
- clear sample preparation record
- raw data availability where possible

## Publication Standard

Do not overclaim. Clearly label:

- predicted
- hypothetical
- candidate
- observed
- replicated
