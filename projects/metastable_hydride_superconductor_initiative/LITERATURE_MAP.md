# Literature Map

## Literature Areas to Cover

### 1. High-Pressure Hydride Superconductivity

**Purpose:** Understand why hydrogen-rich materials can produce high electron-phonon coupling and high predicted or measured superconducting transition temperatures under pressure.

**Key topics:**

- hydrogen-rich lattices
- electron-phonon coupling
- high-pressure synthesis
- clathrate hydrides
- rare-earth hydrides
- transition-metal hydrides

### 2. Metastability and Pressure Quenching

**Purpose:** Understand whether high-pressure or hydrogen-rich phases can be trapped, quenched, or retained outside their equilibrium conditions.

**Key topics:**

- metastable phases
- kinetic barriers
- pressure quenching
- phase retention
- non-equilibrium synthesis

### 3. Magnesium / Iridium / Hydride Chemistry

**Purpose:** Understand known Mg-Ir-H compounds, structural motifs, and possible hydrogen stoichiometries.

**Key topics:**

- Mg₂IrH₅ structure
- transition-metal hydride bonding
- magnesium hydrides
- iridium hydrides
- hydrogen vacancy formation

### 4. Hydrogen Insertion and Vacancy Filling

**Purpose:** Understand chemical and electrochemical methods that might insert hydrogen into existing lattices.

**Key topics:**

- topochemical hydrogenation
- electrochemical hydrogen insertion
- plasma hydrogenation
- chemical hydride routes
- hydrogen diffusion barriers

### 5. Computational Superconductivity Screening

**Purpose:** Identify computational workflow standards.

**Key topics:**

- DFT relaxation
- convex hull / phase stability
- phonon dispersion
- electron-phonon coupling
- Allen-Dynes / McMillan equation
- Quantum ESPRESSO
- ASE
- EPW
- phonopy

### 6. Safety and Replication

**Purpose:** Make sure any experimental roadmap is responsible.

**Key topics:**

- hydrogen safety
- hydride reactivity
- high-pressure cell safety
- toxic or reactive precursors
- independent characterization
- reproducibility standards

## Literature Map Output

For each paper collected later, record:

- title
- authors
- year
- journal
- DOI/link
- material system
- method
- relevance
- key finding
- limitation
- whether it supports or challenges the Mg₂IrH₅ → Mg₂IrH₆ pathway
