# Fundraising Brief

## Project

Metastable Hydride Superconductor Initiative (MHSI)

## Parent Lab

Bowers Frontier Macro Labs

## Mission

Explore whether metastable hydrogen-rich hydride phases can be computationally identified and prioritized for expert experimental validation.

## Why It Matters

Hydrogen-rich superconductors have demonstrated extraordinary potential under high pressure. The next frontier is identifying whether metastable hydride phases can be stabilized, quenched, or accessed at lower pressure or ambient conditions.

## Initial Proof-of-Platform

**Mg₂IrH₅ → Mg₂IrH₆**

This is a speculative candidate pathway used to test the broader platform:

- identify parent hydride,
- propose hydrogen-enriched derivative,
- compute stability,
- compute phonons,
- evaluate electron-phonon coupling,
- assess synthesis feasibility,
- require replication.

## Funding Use

Potential funding would support:

1. computational screening
2. structure generation
3. DFT and phonon calculations
4. literature review
5. expert consultation
6. lab outreach
7. safety review
8. replication planning
9. publication-quality documentation

## Deliverables

- technical brief
- literature map
- computational workflow
- candidate structure files
- DFT input templates
- phonon workflow
- NEB roadmap
- lab outreach package
- safety and replication plan

## Risk Statement

This is high-risk exploratory science. The target phase may be unstable, unsynthesizable, or scientifically uninteresting. The value of the project is in building a rigorous platform for testing metastable hydride hypotheses.

## Scientific Integrity

No superconductivity claim will be made without computational and experimental validation.
