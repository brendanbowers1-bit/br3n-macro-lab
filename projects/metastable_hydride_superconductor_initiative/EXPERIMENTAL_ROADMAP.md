# Experimental Roadmap

## Purpose

This roadmap outlines possible experimental validation steps if computational screening identifies plausible metastable hydride candidates.

This is **not** an instruction to perform synthesis without appropriate laboratory expertise, safety review, institutional approval, and trained personnel.

## Stage 1 — Computational Triage

Before experimental work:

- confirm relaxed candidate structures
- evaluate formation energy
- check phonon stability
- evaluate hydrogen insertion feasibility
- compare against competing phases
- identify safety hazards

## Stage 2 — Expert Review

Before contacting labs:

- prepare technical brief
- prepare structure files
- prepare computational results
- identify uncertainty
- identify hazards
- define characterization needs

## Stage 3 — Synthesis Route Exploration

Potential route classes:

- high-pressure hydrogenation
- pressure-quench synthesis
- topochemical hydrogen insertion
- electrochemical hydrogenation
- plasma hydrogenation
- chemical hydride routes

All routes require expert evaluation.

## Stage 4 — Characterization

Potential characterization:

- X-ray diffraction
- neutron diffraction where hydrogen positions matter
- Raman / IR spectroscopy
- transport measurements
- magnetization
- heat capacity
- pressure-dependent measurements
- isotope substitution if relevant

## Stage 5 — Superconductivity Validation

Any superconductivity claim would require:

- zero resistance
- Meissner effect / magnetic screening
- reproducibility
- structural confirmation
- control samples
- independent lab replication
- exclusion of impurity phases

## Stage 6 — Replication

No claim should be considered validated until independently replicated.
