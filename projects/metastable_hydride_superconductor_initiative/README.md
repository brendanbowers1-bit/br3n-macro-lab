# Metastable Hydride Superconductor Initiative

**Short name:** MHSI  
**Parent lab:** Bowers Frontier Macro Labs

The Metastable Hydride Superconductor Initiative is a speculative materials-science research project under Bowers Frontier Macro Labs.

The initiative studies whether metastable hydrogen-rich hydride phases can be explored through non-equilibrium hydrogen insertion, vacancy filling, pressure-quench chemistry, computational screening, and safety-first experimental roadmapping.

## Flagship System

**Mg₂IrH₅ → Mg₂IrH₆**

The initial proof-of-platform concept focuses on whether Mg₂IrH₅ can be computationally and experimentally evaluated as a hydrogen-deficient parent phase, with Mg₂IrH₆ treated as a **hypothetical metastable hydrogen-inserted target** — not a verified superconducting material.

## Core Research Question

Can non-equilibrium hydrogen insertion or vacancy filling create metastable hydride phases that are electronically, dynamically, and chemically plausible enough to justify experimental synthesis attempts?

## Research Themes

- metastable hydrides
- hydrogen vacancy filling
- non-equilibrium synthesis
- pressure-quench chemistry
- computational screening
- DFT stability analysis
- phonon stability
- electron-phonon coupling
- ambient-pressure superconductivity pathways
- safety and replication standards

## Scientific Disclaimer

This initiative is **speculative research planning**. It does not claim discovery of a superconductor, does not claim verified room-temperature superconductivity, and does not claim that Mg₂IrH₆ has been experimentally synthesized or validated.

All candidate phases require computational validation, experimental feasibility review, safety review, and independent replication.
