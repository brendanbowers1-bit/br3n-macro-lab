# Literature Map — Value Survival Index

This document situates the VSI within prior research. The project **measures** cross-border value loss under stated assumptions; it does not claim to replace established literatures.

---

## 1. Remittance cost measurement

**Prior literature:** World Bank RPW, Beck & Martinez Peria (2009), Orozco & Fedewa, CGAP payment cost studies.

**What VSI adds:** Combines RPW fee and FX margin with timing, inflation, and payout channels into a single survival metric (VSI) with three specifications (Core, Risk-Adjusted, Extended).

**Data sources:** World Bank RPW.

**Limitations:** RPW covers sampled providers and send amounts; may not represent all rails.

---

## 2. FX margins and payment frictions

**Prior literature:** Payment economics, cross-border payment cost decomposition, correspondent banking frictions.

**What VSI adds:** Transparent decomposition with source tags; RPW total cost reconciliation in robustness checks.

**Data sources:** RPW, manual payout defaults.

**Limitations:** Payout friction defaults are assumptions pending corridor validation.

---

## 3. Real exchange rates and purchasing power

**Prior literature:** PPP, real effective exchange rates, remittance real value studies.

**What VSI adds:** Corridor-level “value per $100 sent” framing with inflation erosion channel.

**Data sources:** IMF FX, World Bank inflation.

**Limitations:** Short transfer windows may understate inflation channel for fast transfers.

---

## 4. Central-bank credibility

**Prior literature:** Inflation targeting credibility, exchange-rate regime literature.

**What VSI adds:** Currency trust sub-index (extended specification only).

**Data sources:** Macro panel, sovereignty estimates.

**Limitations:** Trust discount is model-based, not market-observed.

---

## 5. Currency crises and institutional quality

**Prior literature:** Reinhart-Rogoff, EMBI spreads, institutional quality indices.

**What VSI adds:** Event-study framework for depreciation episodes (exploratory).

**Data sources:** IMF FX, FRED DXY.

**Limitations:** Thin panels limit event-window inference.

---

## 6. Dollar dominance and international currency hierarchy

**Prior literature:** Eichengreen, Gopinath et al., BIS triennial survey.

**What VSI adds:** Dollar dependency drag in VSI_EXTENDED only.

**Data sources:** BIS turnover, sovereignty manual file.

**Limitations:** Extended specification — excluded from baseline VSI_CORE.

---

## 7. Remittances and household welfare

**Prior literature:** Yang (2008), Ambrosius & Cuecuecha, household survey evidence.

**What VSI adds:** Aggregate corridor welfare loss estimates (flows × loss rate).

**Data sources:** KNOMAD flows, VSI outputs.

**Limitations:** Does not replace household survey evidence; aggregate arithmetic only.

---

## 8. Market microstructure and FX liquidity

**Prior literature:** BIS turnover, FX market depth, bid-ask spread studies.

**What VSI adds:** Volatility exposure penalty with hedge-access parameter.

**Data sources:** Lab FX cache, BIS.

**Limitations:** Volatility penalty weight is a sensitivity parameter (0.05–0.20).

---

## What this project does NOT claim

- It does **not** claim to invent remittance cost measurement (RPW and prior work exist).
- It does **not** claim to **prove** causal welfare effects without identification.
- It does **not** claim to predict FX rates or provide investment advice.
- It does **not** claim philosophical components (trust, dollar drag) are directly observed in baseline VSI.
- It does **not** replace household survey evidence on remittance use.
- It does **not** provide trading signals or portfolio recommendations.
