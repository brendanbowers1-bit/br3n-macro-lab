# Abstract

This paper proposes a corridor-level measurement framework—the **Value Survival Index (VSI)**—that estimates how much economic value survives when money crosses from one monetary trust system into another. For each remittance corridor, the framework combines observed remittance fees and foreign-exchange margins (World Bank Remittance Prices Worldwide) with transparent adjustments for transfer timing, inflation erosion, payout frictions, and—under an extended specification—volatility exposure, dollar dependency, and currency-trust proxies.

The baseline index (VSI_CORE) restricts itself to components that can be anchored in observed or standard macro data; extended components are reported separately to preserve empirical discipline. We provide sensitivity analysis across conservative, baseline, and severe parameterizations, robustness checks on corridor rankings, and a data-quality rubric that tags official versus assumed inputs.

Under this specification, the VSI **measures** cross-border value leakage; it does **not** establish causal welfare effects without further identification. The framework is intended for economists, FX analysts, and policy researchers who require transparent, replicable corridor comparisons. This is research infrastructure—not investment advice, not a trading signal, and not an FX forecast.
