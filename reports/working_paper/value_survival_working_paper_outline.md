# The Value Survival Index: Measuring How Much Value Survives When Money Crosses Borders

## Working paper outline

### 1. Introduction
- Motivation: FX as daily auction of global trust
- Research question: For every $100 sent, how much survives as usable purchasing power?
- Contribution: Transparent, replicable corridor-level measurement framework
- What we do not claim (see Limitations)

### 2. Related literature
- Remittance cost measurement (RPW)
- Real exchange rates and purchasing power
- Dollar dominance and currency credibility
- See LITERATURE_MAP.md

### 3. Measurement framework
- VSI = 100 × Real Usable Value Delivered / Original Value Sent
- Three specifications: VSI_CORE, VSI_RISK_ADJUSTED, VSI_EXTENDED
- Component decomposition with source traceability

### 4. Data
- World Bank RPW, KNOMAD, IMF FX, WB macro, BIS turnover
- Data quality rubric (0–100)
- Manual/assumption inputs clearly labeled

### 5. Empirical results
- Corridor rankings under baseline specification
- Sensitivity analysis (conservative / baseline / severe)
- Robustness checks (rank stability)
- Exploratory associations (not causal)

### 6. Aggregate welfare implications
- KNOMAD-weighted value loss estimates
- Identification limits

### 7. Conclusion
- Policy and research uses
- Next steps for publication-quality identification
