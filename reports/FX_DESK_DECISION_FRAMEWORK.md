# Cross-Border Payments FX Desk Decision Framework

Bowers Frontier Macro Labs is an independent research project. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by any employer, financial institution, payment company, trading platform, or data vendor.

Bowers Frontier Macro Labs studies FX as the real-time nervous system of global money movement.

The lab is designed to help analyze how currency regimes, payment corridors, customer flows, hedge costs, liquidity, and operational constraints interact in a cross-border payments environment.

The goal is not to predict FX with certainty. The goal is to improve decision quality around exposure, hedging, pricing, liquidity, and risk governance.

**Research only. Not investment advice. Not a trading instruction. Does not replace policy, approvals, or compliance review.**

This research is for education, analysis, and risk-framing only. It does not place trades.

## The Hardest Questions an FX Desk Must Answer

### 1. What is our true real-time FX exposure?

A desk needs to know exposure by:

- currency
- corridor
- legal entity
- value date
- counterparty
- bank account
- payout obligation
- customer flow
- prefunding balance
- settlement timing

**Core question:** What is our true net exposure by currency, value date, corridor, entity, and counterparty?

### 2. Should we hedge now, later, partially, or not at all?

A hedge can reduce risk but also create cost, slippage, forward-point drag, and over-hedging.

**Core question:** When does hedging reduce risk, and when does it just add churn?

### 3. What FX price or spread should customers receive?

Pricing must balance:

- market FX rate
- corridor competition
- customer sensitivity
- local currency volatility
- hedge cost
- transaction fee strategy
- agent economics
- liquidity risk
- local regulations

**Core question:** How do we quote competitively without underpricing FX risk?

### 4. How do we handle exotic, illiquid, or controlled currencies?

Some currencies have:

- capital controls
- official vs executable rates
- parallel-market pressure
- NDF markets
- local liquidity constraints
- trapped cash risk

**Core question:** Can we actually source the local currency at the rate we quote?

### 5. How much local currency should be prefunded?

Too little prefunding creates payout failures and emergency trades.
Too much prefunding creates trapped cash, devaluation risk, capital controls, and opportunity cost.

**Core question:** How much local currency should be held before demand arrives?

### 6. Which bank or liquidity provider should get the trade?

Best price is not always best execution.

Consider:

- spread
- settlement reliability
- counterparty limits
- local market access
- documentation
- sanctions screening
- credit exposure
- operational reliability

**Core question:** Is the cheapest quote also the safest quote?

### 7. How do we manage value-date and settlement mismatch?

FX risk comes from timing:

- customer pays today
- payout may happen immediately
- bank settlement may be T+1/T+2
- holidays differ by country
- local markets close
- funding arrives late
- weekend gap risk

**Core question:** Do we have currency and cash in the right place, on the right value date, before customers need it?

### 8. How do we manage forward points and carry?

Hedging is not free. Forward rates embed interest-rate differentials.

**Core question:** Is the forward hedge protecting us, or is the carry/forward cost too expensive relative to the risk?

### 9. What happens during a shock?

Events:

- elections
- devaluations
- central-bank intervention
- sanctions
- war
- banking crisis
- capital controls
- flash crashes
- liquidity freezes
- weekend gaps
- inflation shocks

**Core question:** What do we do when the market moves sharply and liquidity disappears?

### 10. How do we separate customer-flow hedging from unauthorized speculation?

A payments FX desk should hedge real exposure, not become a hedge fund.

**Core question:** Are we hedging exposure, or accidentally taking a directional trading view?

## The Most Important Strategic Decision

**Question:** How much FX risk should the company keep versus pass through to customers?

A company can manage FX risk through:

- customer pricing
- FX spread
- transaction fees
- hedging
- prefunding
- payout currency terms
- bank relationships
- corridor limits
- treasury policy

## Bowers Frontier Macro Labs Research Translation

The lab should turn the desk questions into testable research modules:

1. When should the desk not hedge?
2. Which corridors are vulnerable to flow-pressure shocks?
3. When should customer spreads widen?
4. When is forward hedging too expensive?
5. Which currencies need prefunding buffers?
6. When does random walk remain the right assumption?
7. When does high-volatility trend mean danger rather than opportunity?
8. How do we distinguish exposure hedging from directional speculation?

The deepest question for the lab is:

**How do we turn millions of small customer transfers into a real-time map of global currency pressure, without over-hedging noise or taking unauthorized directional risk?**
