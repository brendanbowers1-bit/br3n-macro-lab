# Unanswered FX Questions

## Bowers Frontier Macro Labs — FX Lab

FX Lab studies one of the hardest problems in finance:

Why are exchange rates so hard to forecast, yet sometimes structured enough to matter for trading, hedging, and risk decisions?

The lab is currently focused on a narrower and more practical claim:

A model can fail as a price forecast but still improve hedge governance.

## Priority Research Lanes

1. Forecast Failure and Hedge Usefulness
2. Regime-Dependent UIP
3. Carry Fragility
4. Public Flow Proxies
5. R1 vs R2 Trend Quality

## Current Flagship Question

Can FX regime models fail to beat random walk as forecasts but still improve hedge-governance decisions?

## Working Thesis

FX markets may be mostly random-walk-like as price processes, but not all FX decisions are price forecasts.

Regime, carry, news, flow, and stress variables may fail to predict exchange rates directly while still improving hedge governance, risk escalation, and decision discipline.

## Claim Discipline

The lab does not claim:

- FX prediction certainty
- random walk disproven
- guaranteed trading returns
- live-trading readiness

The lab does claim:

- these questions can be tested systematically
- forecast accuracy, trading P&L, and hedge usefulness should be evaluated separately
- hedge-governance usefulness may survive when prediction fails
